Show Low Arizona

A small town nested in the Arizona white mountains. A beautiful town with the environment the locals called "the high desert". Judging by the sheer size of the town, you may think there is not much to do but you would be wrong. If you love hiking and seeing pre-historic sites, this is the place for you! You will have to do some driving to see these magnificent sites but it is definitely worth it!

Things to see near Show Low

  1. The Painted Desert
  2. The Petrified Forest
  3. Newspaper Rock

Eureka Springs Arkansas

Just a 5 hour drive south from my hometown of Saint Charles is Eureka Springs Arkansas. Never heard of such a place until a friend of mine posted pictures of the town on Facebook. After seeing how beautiful it was, I had to see it for myself. The town had me in awe. It was like taking a trip back into time. The Native inhabitants claimed the area had healing springs which result in settlers flocking to the area to be healed. It is now a tourist destination. All of the buildings have been restored from the early 1900's with keeping the victorian style in mind. Every house, building and hotel had it's own unique charm. I was shocked I didn't know such a place existed. They have festivals every weekend suited for anyones' liking. If you happen to find yourself there, I recommend doing their trolley tour. There's many more things to see in Eureka Springs but never enough time!

What to see in Eureka Springs

  1. The Basin Hotel
  2. The Palace Hotel
  3. The Rogue Manor
  4. The Grotto

Tom Sauk Mountain

This area has a lot of History. I mainly went here for the hiking of Tom Sauk mountain and found a surprise in the process. The beautiful Mina Sauk falls. A mile and a half hike or so on the Tom Sauk trail will bring you to Mina Falls. There's a lot of rock climbing so watch your step and bring plenty of water. You can also do a 13 mile hike down to Johnson shut-ins.

What to see at Tom Sauk Mountain

  1. Mina Sauk Falls
  2. Johnson shut-ins

Alley Springs

If you love going on float trips and are a seasoned floater, you need to make a trip down to Alley Springs. The Jacks fork river is stunning and can be treacherous at times. The river is fed by springs giving it a gorgeous crystal blue color. The water is also very refreshing during a hot summer day. You will see Great Blue herons, King Fishers and all types of turtles hanging out on the banks of the Jacks fork. I even saw several Bald Eagles circling above the tree line. I highly recommend to float during the week when there far less floaters on the water.

What to see in Alley Springs

  1. Jacks Fork River
  2. Alley Mill
